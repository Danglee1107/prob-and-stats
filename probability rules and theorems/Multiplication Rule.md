# Multiplication Rule

## Definition

The **Multiplication Rule** computes the probability that two events occur together.

$$
P(A \cap B)=P(A \mid B)P(B)
$$

Equivalent form:

$$
P(A \cap B)=P(B \mid A)P(A)
$$

---

## Interpretation

The probability of both events happening equals:

1. Probability one event happens
2. Times probability the other event happens afterward

---

## Example

Suppose:

$$
P(B)=0.7
$$

$$
P(A \mid B)=0.5
$$

Then:

$$
P(A \cap B)=0.5 \times 0.7
$$

$$
=0.35
$$
