# Independence

## Definition

Two events are **independent** if one event does not affect the probability of the other.

For independent events:

$$
P(A \cap B)=P(A)P(B)
$$

Equivalent condition:

$$
P(A \mid B)=P(A)
$$

---

## Interpretation

Knowing that event $B$ occurred gives no additional information about event $A$.

---

## Example

Suppose:

- Probability of flipping heads:

$$
P(H)=0.5
$$

- Probability of rolling a 6:

$$
P(6)=\frac{1}{6}
$$

These events are independent.

Therefore:

$$
P(H \cap 6)=0.5 \times \frac{1}{6}
$$

$$
=\frac{1}{12}
$$
