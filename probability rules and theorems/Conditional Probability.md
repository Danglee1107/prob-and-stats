# Conditional Probability

## Definition

Conditional probability measures the probability of an event occurring **given that another event has already occurred**.

$$
P(A \mid B)=\frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}
$$

Where:

- $P(A \mid B)$ = probability of $A$ given $B$
- $P(A \cap B)$ = probability both occur
- $P(B)$ = probability of event $B$

Condition:

$$
P(B) > 0
$$

---

## Interpretation

The condition event $B$ changes the sample space.

Instead of considering all outcomes, we only consider outcomes where $B$ happens.

---

## Example

Suppose:

- Probability a student passes math:

$$
P(M)=0.7
$$

- Probability a student passes both math and physics:

$$
P(M \cap P)=0.5
$$

Find probability that a student passes math given they passed physics:

Assume:

$$
P(P)=0.8
$$

Then:

$$
P(M \mid P)=\frac{0.5}{0.8}
$$

$$
=0.625
$$
